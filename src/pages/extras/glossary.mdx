## Terms

### Beer Bong
A funnel with a long tube used to rapidly consume drink poured into it.

### Beer Jacket
The “invisible coat” that keeps you from feeling cold after heavy drinking.

### Body Shot
Partner holds lime; salt placed on a body part. Lick salt, take shot, bite lime.

### Chaser
A softer drink immediately after a harsh shot (juice, soda, beer).

### Chug
To drink rapidly without pausing for breath; often chanted by others.

### Double Parked
Holding/drinking two different drinks simultaneously.

### Fingers
A “measurement” using horizontal finger widths of liquid in a glass.

### Flaming Shot
High‑proof shot is lit then consumed (high risk; not recommended).

### Ice Luge
Alcohol poured down a carved ice channel into a waiting mouth.

### Inverted Bong
Beer bong consumed while being held upside‑down.

### Keg Stand
Held upside‑down atop a keg while the tap is poured until you signal stop.

### Minesweeping
Taking an unattended (often unfinished) drink as your own.

### Penguin Feed
Pouring alcohol from one mouth to another, optionally removing the cup.

### Pre‑Drinking / Pre‑Gaming
Drinking before going out to accelerate reaching a buzz.

### Shotgun
Piercing low side of a can, placing mouth over hole, opening top, and chugging.

### Strawpedo
Using a straw in a bottle to allow air in so liquid pours faster while chugging.

### Suicide Shot
Snort salt, squeeze lime in eye, then take the shot (dangerous; not advised).

### Yard
A very tall yard‑long glass holding ~2–3 pints with a bulb at the base.
